I know personally that Toby Fox's work has influenced me more, and been a bigger source of inspiration than any other piece of media. I don't think Undertale or Deltarune are neccesarily the best games I've ever played, but they're my personal favorites for how they made me feel while playing and how that feeling stuck with me even long after I put them down. Speaking on the "after I put them down" part, it's also hard to process how long this series has been a part of my life. I was a kid when Undertale originally released. I even have nostaligc memories of listening to the soundtrack in my bed, not wanting to sleep because I had school the next day. Now, seven years later, its an even bigger part of my life, and I anticipate its going to stay that way for years to come.

[IF YOU'RE NEW HERE]

If you've read up to this point and aren't quite sure what "Everytale" is, allow me to clarify. Everytale is an online Undertale fangame I'm currently developing. The game combines social game elements (i.e. VRChat) with Undertale style turn-based combat for PvE and PvP. Players can hang out, role-play, chat, and battle in an Undertale/Deltarune style world, together! The game will also feature built-in mod tools so players can import/create their own custom characters to play and fight as, all completely cross-platform. If you want more details on the game, you can read the game design document --here [INSERT LINK]--.




[CATCHING UP]

So, what's been going on with the project since I made those first posts a month ago? Originally, even after I decided I was going to commit to this project, I actually didn't plan to do much. Classes were close to starting back up again at that point, and not wanting to overwhelm myself, I decided I wouldn't work on the project for a few weeks so I could get into the groove of things in my personal life. Then, after those few weeks, I would get the project files set up and ready to develop with. I would follow through... on the second part of this plan. As for the "not working on it for a few weeks" part? Turns out when it comes to game development stuff I can't help myself. I ended up creating prototypes for multiple systems that will be in Everytale to help myself decide how I should implement them in the real project. I named the Godot project "Dynamic Resources Test" as it was originally meant to only test Godot's resource system, which acts as the foundation for modding capibilities within Everytale. Howver, after I successfully prototyped that system, I qucikly found myself wanting to prototype other features as well, and the project quickly grew well past its original scope. This has given me a variety of in-engine previews to show you!
